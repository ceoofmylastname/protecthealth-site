Going self-employed means losing the benefits packet — and discovering nobody hands you a map. Here is the map: every real coverage path for 1099 contractors, freelancers, and self-employed professionals in Nevada, and how to know which one is yours.

## What are the four coverage paths?

**Path 1 — Marketplace plans with tax credits.** The workhorse. Roughly 5 million self-employed workers and small-business owners used ACA marketplaces in 2025. Premium tax credits key off *net* income after business expenses, so plenty of self-employed households qualify without realizing it. Details in the [Silver State Exchange guide](/blog-post/silver-state-health-insurance-exchange).

**Path 2 — Genuine PPO access.** For those who need broad networks: frequent travelers, out-of-state specialists, specific doctors. Availability depends on the individual situation, which is why "everyone should get a PPO" and "PPOs don't exist anymore" are both wrong.

**Path 3 — Small-group opportunities.** Even one W-2 employee (who is not a spouse) can change the landscape. Teams, offices, and family businesses are structured a hundred ways, and some structures unlock group options.

**Path 4 — Tax-advantaged structures.** Arrangements like an [ICHRA](/blog-post/what-is-an-ichra) or a Section 105 plan can turn health costs into business-advantaged spending when the entity type and setup fit. These depend heavily on structure — sole proprietor versus LLC versus S-corp — and belong in a conversation that includes a tax professional.

## How does business structure change the answer?

| Structure | What it can change |
|-----------|--------------------|
| Sole proprietor | Above-the-line premium deduction; spouse-employee strategies possible |
| LLC | Depends on tax election; flexibility on reimbursement arrangements |
| S-corp | Premium deduction runs through payroll; >2% owners face special ICHRA rules |
| Any + W-2 staff | Small-group and reimbursement structures come into play |

The pattern to notice: **the coverage conversation and the business-structure conversation are the same conversation.** That is why blanket advice from a Facebook comment section is worth exactly what it costs.
