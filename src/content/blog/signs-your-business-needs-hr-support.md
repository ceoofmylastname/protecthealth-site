Quick question for business owners: who's your HR department? If the answer is "me, I guess," this checklist is for you. It works fine — right up until it doesn't.

## What are the seven signs?

**1. There's no employee handbook.** Policies exist verbally, which means they exist differently in every employee's memory. The first dispute makes this expensive.

**2. Onboarding lives in someone's head.** When the person who "knows how we do it" is on vacation, new hires learn by osmosis. Documentation turns tribal knowledge into a system.

**3. Payroll happens on Sunday night.** Double-checking deductions, chasing a W-4, hoping the tax filing is right — every week, forever. 70% of owners lose more than a week a month to this category of work.

**4. Employee files are in three drawers and an inbox.** I-9s, W-4s, reviews, and write-ups scattered across paper and email are a compliance audit waiting to go badly.

**5. Compliance questions get answered by guessing.** 51% of small businesses say compliance actively holds back growth. Guessing works until the first letter arrives.

**6. Good people keep leaving for "better packages."** Salary-only offers lose to benefits, retirement plans, and employers that have their act together. See [the small business health insurance guide](/blog-post/small-business-health-insurance) for how first-time benefit programs actually get built.

**7. Growth is breaking the systems.** New location, fifth employee, tenth employee — the spreadsheet and shoebox that got the business here stop working. Growing pains are the best problem to have, but they are a problem.

## What's the fix — hire an HR department?

Almost never. The fix is **reachable HR guidance**: handbooks, onboarding processes, compliance support, and documentation, delivered as a service instead of a salary.
