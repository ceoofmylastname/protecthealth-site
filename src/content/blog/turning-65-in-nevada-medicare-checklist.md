Medicare has exactly one enrollment window where every door is open and nothing requires a health question. Most people find out about it after it closes. If a 65th birthday is on the calendar in the next year, this checklist is the difference between choosing a path and being assigned one.

## What happens during the 7-month window?

The Initial Enrollment Period runs 7 months: the 3 months before the birthday month, the birthday month, and the 3 months after. Enrolling in the 3 early months means coverage starts the first day of the birthday month. Enrolling late in the window can delay the start date.

Already drawing Social Security? Parts A and B arrive automatically — watch the mail for the card. Not drawing yet? Nothing is automatic. Enrollment happens through Social Security, online or by appointment, and nobody sends a reminder.

## What does missing the window cost?

The Part B late penalty is 10% of the premium for every full 12-month period of delay, and it never goes away. Delay three years without qualifying coverage and every future Part B premium costs 30% more, for life. Part D (drug coverage) has its own smaller-but-permanent penalty that accrues monthly. These are the most avoidable permanent costs in retirement, and avoiding them takes one afternoon.

The main legitimate reason to delay: still working at 65 with qualifying employer coverage (generally from an employer with 20+ employees), which earns a Special Enrollment Period later. The word "qualifying" carries weight — COBRA and retiree coverage do not count, a detail that catches people every year.

## What is the real decision at 65?

Enrollment is paperwork. The decision is the coverage path:

**Path 1: Original Medicare + Medigap + Part D.** See any doctor in America who takes Medicare, with a supplement absorbing most cost-sharing. Higher monthly premium, near-zero surprise bills.

**Path 2: Medicare Advantage.** Low or zero additional premium, usually with drug coverage and extras bundled in, in exchange for a network and plan rules.

Here's why the decision has a deadline attached: the 6-month Medigap open enrollment window that starts with Part B is the one guaranteed chance to buy any supplement with no health questions. In Nevada, applying after that window generally means medical underwriting — and a health history can mean denial. The Advantage path stays open every year. The Medigap path may not.

## The checklist, in order

Three months before the birthday month: confirm enrollment status with Social Security, decide on delaying (only with verified qualifying employer coverage), and start comparing the two paths. During the window: enroll in A and B, pick the path, and if choosing Medigap, buy inside the 6-month underwriting-free window. After coverage starts: confirm every current doctor and prescription against the chosen plan, and calendar the annual October 15 to December 7 review period.

One afternoon of decisions, made on time, in the right order. That is the entire game.
