The question comes up in every Realtor Facebook group, usually right after someone gets a renewal letter. The short answer disappoints; the long answer has more paths than most agents have heard of.

## Why doesn't the brokerage plan cover agents?

Group health insurance is a W-2 employee benefit, and Realtors are 1099 independent contractors — each agent is a business, not an employee. That structural fact is also why 10-15% of Realtors carry no coverage at all, per NAR surveys: nobody hands a 1099 agent a benefits packet.

## What are the real exceptions and paths?

Teams with W-2 staff can carry small-group plans for those employees. Some associations offer member coverage options worth comparing. And an agent whose own entity employs a W-2 worker may unlock small-group or reimbursement structures — including the arrangements covered in [the ICHRA guide](/blog-post/what-is-an-ichra) and the [Section 105 explainer](/qa/what-is-a-section-105-plan).

For most agents, the workhorse remains an individual marketplace plan with premium tax credits — commission income counts *after* expenses, so agents qualify more often than they assume. The full landscape, including PPO access and structure-dependent options, is mapped in the [Realtor health insurance guide](/blog-post/realtor-health-insurance-guide).
