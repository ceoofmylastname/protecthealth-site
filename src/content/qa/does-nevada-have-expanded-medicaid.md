Yes — and for self-employed Nevadans with a lean year, that one word changes what the safety net looks like.

## What did Medicaid expansion change in Nevada?

Before expansion, adult Medicaid required fitting a category — pregnancy, disability, caretaker status. Expansion made income the test:

1. **Income-based eligibility** for adults, measured as modified adjusted gross income against the federal poverty level.
2. **No coverage gap.** In non-expansion states, some adults earn too much for Medicaid and too little for subsidies. Nevada's programs meet edge to edge.
3. **Year-round enrollment.** Medicaid has no open enrollment window; eligibility can begin any month income qualifies.

## How does Nevada route people to the right program?

Through one application. Nevada Health Link — the marketplace operated by the [Silver State Health Insurance Exchange](/blog-post/silver-state-health-insurance-exchange) — screens household income and sends each applicant to the right shelf: Medicaid below the threshold, premium-tax-credit marketplace plans above it. Nobody has to diagnose their own eligibility before applying, which is the quiet genius of the design. Households near the line get whichever answer the income estimate supports.

## Why does this matter for 1099 and commission earners?

Because self-employed income moves, and the system is built for movement in both directions. A rough year can drop a Realtor into Medicaid eligibility mid-year — enrollable immediately, no window required. A strong year that ends Medicaid eligibility is itself a qualifying life event, opening a special enrollment period where marketplace subsidies take over. The moving part worth managing is the income estimate itself; when projections involve a Schedule C, a licensed tax professional belongs in the conversation alongside the insurance strategy.
