Every state has a front door to ACA coverage. Nevada built its own — and knowing how it works saves both money and confusion.

## What does Nevada Health Link actually do?

Nevada Health Link is the shopping, comparison, and enrollment platform for individual and family health insurance in Nevada, operated by the [Silver State Health Insurance Exchange](/blog-post/silver-state-health-insurance-exchange), a state agency. Three functions matter most:

1. **Plan marketplace** — every ACA-compliant individual plan from participating carriers, side by side.
2. **Subsidy engine** — the only channel where premium tax credits and Silver-plan cost-sharing reductions apply.
3. **Eligibility router** — the application screens household income and routes Medicaid-eligible applicants automatically, since Nevada expanded Medicaid.

## Is Nevada Health Link the government, or an insurance company?

Neither sells the insurance and both descriptions miss it. The Exchange is a state agency running a marketplace; the plans on the shelf come from private carriers, priced and regulated the same as anywhere else. The practical consequence: a plan costs the same through Nevada Health Link, through a carrier directly, or through a broker — except that subsidies exist only on the marketplace side. For any household that qualifies for a credit, buying off-marketplace means voluntarily overpaying.

## How does enrollment through the platform work?

The rhythm is annual. Open enrollment runs November 1 through January 15, with a December 15 cutoff for January 1 coverage. Outside the window, qualifying life events — coverage loss, marriage, a birth, a move into Nevada — open 60-day special enrollment periods. The application itself turns on one number above all: the household income estimate, which drives the tax credit, the cost-sharing reductions, and the Medicaid screen all at once. Estimating it carefully is the whole game.
